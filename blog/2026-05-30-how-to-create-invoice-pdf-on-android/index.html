How to create invoice PDF on Android

Quick answer

You can create a clear PDF invoice, estimate, quote, or receipt on an Android phone by entering the client details, adding service line items, checking the totals, exporting the document as a PDF, and sharing it through WhatsApp, email, or the Android share sheet. This workflow is useful when you finish a job away from a desk and need to send a professional document before the details are forgotten.

This guide is written for a freelancer who wants a simple mobile workflow. It uses the approved features of Doclira: create invoices, estimates, quotes, and receipts; save clients; save services or items; export PDFs; share through compatible Android apps; track paid and unpaid documents; work offline; and use basic features without login.

Who this is for

This is for contractors, freelancers, cleaners, electricians, plumbers, mobile mechanics, tutors, photographers, and small service businesses that create documents after real work. A phone-based process is especially helpful when the job happens at a client site, in a shop, during a home visit, or while traveling between appointments.

The goal is not to replace professional advice or complex back-office systems. The goal is to keep everyday billing documents organized and easy to share. If your work is simple service billing, a mobile PDF workflow can save time and reduce handwritten mistakes.

What the document should include

A useful service document should usually include your business name, client name, document number, date, service description, quantity, price, total, notes, and payment instructions if you want to add them. For a receipt, include enough detail to show what was paid. For an estimate or quote, include the proposed work and clear notes so the client understands what is included.

Keep the wording plain. A client should be able to open the PDF and understand what work was done, what amount is listed, and how to contact you if there is a question. Avoid vague line items such as “work” or “repair” when a more specific phrase would help.

Step-by-step guide

  1. Open the Android app and choose the document type you need: invoice, estimate, quote, or receipt.
  2. Add or select the client. Saving repeat clients helps you avoid typing the same phone number, address, or name again.
  3. Add service or item lines. Use short descriptions that match the actual work.
  4. Enter quantity and price for each line. Review the total before exporting.
  5. Add notes if needed. Keep notes practical, such as job location, service date, or simple payment instructions.
  6. Preview the PDF and check spelling, amounts, and client details.
  7. Save the document so you can find it later.
  8. Share the PDF through WhatsApp, email, or another compatible Android app.
  9. Mark the document paid or unpaid when that status is useful for your follow-up.

Example line items

Here are simple examples you can adapt:

  • Service call, 1 job, 500
  • Labor charges, 2 hours, 1,200
  • Material supplied, 1 set, 800
  • Deep cleaning, 1 job, 2,500
  • Fan installation, 2 units, 1,200

The exact prices depend on your own work and market. The important part is that each line item is understandable. If the client reads the PDF a week later, the document should still make sense.

Common mistakes to avoid

Do not send a document before checking the client name and amount. Small typing errors can create extra messages and confusion. Do not use one unclear line item for several different services if the client expects detail. Do not rely only on chat history when a PDF record would be easier to find later. Do not make promises in the document that are outside your actual app features or business process.

Another common mistake is waiting until the end of the day to create every invoice or receipt. When possible, create the document soon after the job. The service details are fresh, and the client is already available to receive the PDF.

Practical mobile workflow

For repeat jobs, save common clients and services in the app. For one-time jobs, create the document from a simple template-like flow: client, line items, notes, preview, export, share. For estimates and quotes, keep the description focused on the proposed work. For receipts, keep the document focused on what was paid.

Offline access is useful when you are at a site with weak internet. You can prepare the document on the phone, then share it when connectivity is available. This makes the workflow less dependent on a laptop, office printer, or browser login.
